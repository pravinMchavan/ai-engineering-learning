Central Limit Theorem

Definition:
The Central Limit Theorem (CLT) states that the sampling distribution of the sample mean approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases, regardless of the shape of the original population distribution.

Statement:
If X1, X2, ..., Xn are random samples from a population with mean μ and finite variance σ², then the sample mean X̄ has an approximately normal distribution with mean μ and variance σ²/n for large n.

Assumptions:
- Random sampling
- Independent observations
- Finite population variance
- Sample size n ≥ 30 (rule of thumb)

Solved Example:
Population: Exponential distribution with mean μ = 2
Sample size n = 50
Sample mean X̄ ≈ Normal(μ=2, σ/√n)
For large n, the distribution of sample means is normal.

Explanation:
The CLT allows us to use normal distribution approximations for inference, even when the population is not normal. It's fundamental for confidence intervals and hypothesis testing.
