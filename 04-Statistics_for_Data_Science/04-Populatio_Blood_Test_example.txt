Population and Blood Test Example Using Probability

Context:
A population is the full group we want to study. A sample is a smaller subset taken from the population.

Problem:
A city has 10,000 people. A health survey finds that 1,200 people have high cholesterol. A blood test is randomly given to one person from the city.

Probability Goal:
Find the probability that the randomly selected person has high cholesterol.

Solution:
- Total population = 10,000 people
- Number with high cholesterol = 1,200
- Probability = favorable outcomes / total outcomes

Formula:
P(high cholesterol) = 1,200 / 10,000 = 0.12

Interpretation:
There is a 12% chance that a randomly selected person from the city has high cholesterol.

Extended Example:
If 50 people are randomly chosen for the blood test, the expected number with high cholesterol is:
Expected count = 50 * 0.12 = 6 people

Explanation:
- This problem uses probability for a population event.
- The probability is based on the known number of cases in the entire population.
- In practice, if we do not know the full population, we use a sample and estimate the probability using sample proportions.

Key Terms:
- Population: the complete group (10,000 people)
- Event: the selected person has high cholesterol
- Probability: likelihood of the event happening
