Variance

Definition:
Variance measures the spread or dispersion of data points around the mean. It quantifies how much the values in a data set vary from the average.

Formula:
For a population: σ² = Σ(xi - μ)² / N
For a sample: s² = Σ(xi - x̄)² / (n - 1)

Where:
- xi = each data point
- μ = population mean
- x̄ = sample mean
- N = population size
- n = sample size

Solved Example:
Data: 5, 8, 12, 15, 20
Mean (x̄) = (5+8+12+15+20)/5 = 60/5 = 12

Variance calculation:
(5-12)² = 49
(8-12)² = 16
(12-12)² = 0
(15-12)² = 9
(20-12)² = 64
Sum of squared differences = 49 + 16 + 0 + 9 + 64 = 138
Variance = 138 / (5-1) = 138/4 = 34.5

Explanation:
Variance shows how spread out the data are. A higher variance means data points are more spread out from the mean. It is the average of the squared differences from the mean.

Standard Deviation

Definition:
Standard deviation measures the amount of variation or dispersion in a data set. It is the square root of the variance, and it expresses variability in the same units as the data.

Formula:
For a population: σ = √(Σ(xi - μ)² / N)
For a sample: s = √(Σ(xi - x̄)² / (n - 1))

Where:
- xi = each data point
- μ = population mean
- x̄ = sample mean
- N = population size
- n = sample size

Solved Example:
Data: 5, 8, 12, 15, 20
Mean (x̄) = 12
Variance = 34.5
Standard deviation = √34.5 ≈ 5.87

Explanation:
Standard deviation describes how spread out the values are around the mean. A smaller standard deviation means the values are closer to the mean, while a larger standard deviation indicates more spread.

Coefficient of Variation

Definition:
Coefficient of Variation (CV) is a normalized measure of dispersion of a probability distribution or frequency distribution. It shows the size of the standard deviation relative to the mean.

Formula:
CV = (Standard Deviation / Mean) × 100%

Where:
- Standard Deviation = σ or s
- Mean = μ or x̄

Solved Example:
Data: 5, 8, 12, 15, 20
Mean (x̄) = 12
Standard deviation ≈ 5.87
CV = (5.87 / 12) × 100% ≈ 48.9%

Explanation:
The coefficient of variation allows comparison of variability between data sets with different units or different means. A higher CV means greater relative variability, while a lower CV means more consistency relative to the mean.

Covariance

Definition:
Covariance measures how much two random variables change together. A positive covariance indicates that the variables tend to increase or decrease together, while a negative covariance indicates that one variable tends to increase when the other decreases.

Formula:
For population: Cov(X, Y) = Σ[(xi - μx)(yi - μy)] / N
For sample: Cov(X, Y) = Σ[(xi - x̄)(yi - ȳ)] / (n - 1)

Where:
- xi and yi = data points for variables X and Y
- μx, μy = population means of X and Y
- x̄, ȳ = sample means of X and Y
- N = population size
- n = sample size

Solved Example:
X: 2, 4, 6
Y: 3, 5, 7
Mean of X = (2+4+6)/3 = 4
Mean of Y = (3+5+7)/3 = 5

Covariance calculation:
(2-4)(3-5) = 4
(4-4)(5-5) = 0
(6-4)(7-5) = 4
Sum = 4 + 0 + 4 = 8
Population Cov(X, Y) = 8 / 3 ≈ 2.67

Explanation:
Covariance shows the direction of the linear relationship between two variables. 
Positive covariance means the variables move together, 
negative covariance means they move in opposite directions, 
and zero covariance means no linear relationship.
