Confidence Interval

Definition:
A confidence interval is a range of values that is likely to contain the true population parameter with a certain level of confidence.

Formula for Population Mean (σ known):
CI = X̄ ± Z × (σ / √n)

Where:
- X̄ = sample mean
- Z = Z-score for confidence level (e.g., 1.96 for 95%)
- σ = population standard deviation
- n = sample size

Formula for Population Mean (σ unknown):
CI = X̄ ± t × (s / √n)

Where:
- t = t-score from t-distribution
- s = sample standard deviation

Solved Example:
Sample mean X̄ = 50, σ = 10, n = 100, 95% confidence
Z = 1.96
CI = 50 ± 1.96 × (10 / √100) = 50 ± 1.96 × 1 = 50 ± 1.96
So, 48.04 to 51.96

Explanation:
A 95% confidence interval means that if we repeated the sampling many times, 95% of the intervals would contain the true mean. It does not mean the probability that the true mean is in this interval is 95%.

Interpretation:
- Wider intervals indicate more uncertainty.
- Higher confidence levels result in wider intervals.
- Confidence intervals are used for estimation and hypothesis testing.
