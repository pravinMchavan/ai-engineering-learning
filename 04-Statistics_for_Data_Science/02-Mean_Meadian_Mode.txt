Mean, Median, and Mode

1) Mean
Definition:
The mean is the arithmetic average of a data set. It is found by adding all values and dividing by the number of values.

Formula:
Mean (\bar{x}) = (x_1 + x_2 + ... + x_n) / n

Solved Example:
Data: 5, 8, 12, 15, 20
Sum = 5 + 8 + 12 + 15 + 20 = 60
Number of values n = 5
Mean = 60 / 5 = 12

Explanation:
The mean gives a central value for the data set. It is sensitive to extreme values (outliers), so one very large or very small value can affect it.

2) Median
Definition:
The median is the middle value of an ordered data set. If the number of values is even, the median is the average of the two middle values.

Steps:
- Arrange data in ascending order.
- If n is odd, the median is the middle value.
- If n is even, the median is the average of the two middle values.

Solved Example 1 (odd count):
Data: 7, 2, 11, 5, 9
Ordered: 2, 5, 7, 9, 11
Middle value = 7
Median = 7

Solved Example 2 (even count):
Data: 4, 10, 6, 2
Ordered: 2, 4, 6, 10
Middle two values = 4 and 6
Median = (4 + 6) / 2 = 5

Explanation:
The median is useful when the data contain outliers or a skewed distribution because it depends only on the middle position, not on all values.

3) Mode
Definition:
The mode is the value that appears most frequently in a data set. A data set may have one mode, more than one mode, or no mode.

Solved Example 1 (single mode):
Data: 3, 5, 3, 8, 3, 10, 5
Frequencies: 3 appears 3 times, 5 appears 2 times, 8 appears 1 time, 10 appears 1 time
Mode = 3

Solved Example 2 (no mode):
Data: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5
Each value appears once
Mode = none

Solved Example 3 (bimodal):
Data: 2, 4, 4, 6, 6, 8
Frequencies: 4 and 6 both appear twice
Modes = 4 and 6

Explanation:
The mode identifies the most common value(s) in the data. It works for categorical and numeric data and is not affected by extreme values.

Comparison Summary:
- Mean: uses all values and gives an average; best for symmetric distributions.
- Median: middle value; best for skewed distributions or outliers.
- Mode: most frequent value; best for categorical data or identifying common values.
